Pizza

Description

For a classic Margherita pizza, start with a simple dough made from all-purpose flour, water, yeast, salt, and a drizzle of olive oil. Mix the ingredients until they form a smooth, elastic dough, then let it rise for about an hour. While the dough rests, prepare a fresh tomato sauce by simmering crushed tomatoes with garlic, olive oil, and a pinch of salt. Once the dough has doubled in size, stretch it into a thin, round shape, keeping the edges slightly thicker for a perfect crust.

Preheat your oven to its highest setting, ideally 500°F (260°C), and set a pizza stone or baking sheet inside to heat up. Spread the tomato sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges. Tear fresh mozzarella into chunks and scatter them on top, followed by fresh basil leaves and a light drizzle of olive oil. Bake the pizza for about 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden and crispy, and the cheese is bubbling and slightly browned. Once out of the oven, finish it off with a sprinkle of sea salt and more fresh basil if desired. Slice and enjoy!

Ingredients

For the dough:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1 packet (2 ¼ tsp) active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

For the tomato sauce:

  • 1 cup crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Pinch of salt

For the toppings:

  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella, torn into chunks
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Olive oil (for drizzling)
  • Sea salt (optional, for finishing)

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour, warm water, yeast, salt, and olive oil until a smooth, elastic dough forms.
  2. Cover the dough and let it rise for about an hour, or until it doubles in size.
  3. While the dough rises, prepare the tomato sauce by simmering crushed tomatoes, garlic, olive oil, and salt in a small saucepan for about 10-15 minutes. Set aside.
  4. Once the dough has risen, preheat your oven to 500°F (260°C), placing a pizza stone or baking sheet inside to heat up.
  5. On a lightly floured surface, stretch the dough into a thin, round shape, leaving the edges slightly thicker for a nice crust.
  6. Spread the tomato sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  7. Tear the fresh mozzarella into chunks and distribute them evenly on the pizza.
  8. Top with fresh basil leaves and drizzle a small amount of olive oil over the pizza.
  9. Carefully transfer the pizza onto the preheated pizza stone or baking sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling and lightly browned.
  10. Remove from the oven and, if desired, sprinkle with a little sea salt and add more fresh basil for garnish.
  11. Slice and serve hot. Enjoy your homemade Margherita pizza!
